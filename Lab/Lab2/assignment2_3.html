DCS - 2.3 Curve parametrization

The following illustration shows a cycloid curve of radius 30 rolling along the x-axis during 5 periods. We are going to discuss now how to obtain a parametrization for such a curve.

The cycloid is the curve traced by a point on a circle as it rolls along a straight line (the x-axis, in this case) without slipping, as it is suggested in the following animation, which shows a period of the cycloid curve.

A cycloid curve generated by a rolling circle. Author: Zorgit

As we have already said, let's suppose that the line on which the circumference rolls is the x-axis, that the circumference has radius R and that the point whose movement we are going to follow is initially at the origin, O.
